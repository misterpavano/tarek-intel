FDA Proposes New Approval Pathway for Individualized Therapies for Ultra-Rare Diseases

The FDA issued draft guidance establishing a “Plausible Mechanism Framework” to accelerate development and approval of individualized therapies for ultra-rare diseases where randomized controlled trials are not feasible. The framework explicitly covers genome editing and RNA-based therapies and allows for approval based on mechanistic rationale combined with patient-level evidence, marking a fundamental shift in how N-of-1 rare disease programs can reach patients.
💡 What this means
This guidance codifies a path that has been informally negotiated for a handful of ultra-rare cases, including the 2025 CRISPR approval for CPS1 deficiency, into a formal regulatory framework.
